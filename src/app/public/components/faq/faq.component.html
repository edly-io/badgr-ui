FAQ

1. Can badges only be distributed and used in the school context?

"MINT-O-Badges" can be used across institutions: both in a formal school context also outside. It therefore offers the potential to be a bridge between formal, non-formal, and to build informal educational institutions and to make them more interconnected in the sense of networking connect. This is important because learning in the sense of lifelong and networked learning occurs everywhere takes place. Important skills are also acquired in informal areas, which are needed in the spirit of the future Skills can be of great importance.

2. Which level (A1 to C2) of the badges describes which level?

The awarding of badges is based on the level of independence and the level of complexity Skill. We are guided by the 6 competency levels of Digcomp Edu.


  • A1: Beginner - Initial knowledge has been acquired but cannot yet be applied become.
  • A2: Explorer - Basic knowledge and skills are available with which Further knowledge can be acquired in a self-directed manner. This can be used in standard situations but it is not enough to be able to solve unexpected problems independently.
  • B1: Insiders - Acquired knowledge and skills can be applied to unknown people Situations are transferred.
  • B2: Expert - The knowledge is used to understand connections and possible alternatives recognize. In addition, there is the competence to independently acquire and develop additional knowledge Sharing knowledge (digitally).
  • C1: Leader - The developed knowledge and skills are based on Experiences used and applied innovatively.
  • C2: Pioneer - Through the acquired skills you can develop your own skills and New definitions are carried out and existing procedures are critically examined.

3. Why are badges with a six-point scale used here and not traditional school grades?

“MINT-O-Badges” are based on the portfolio approach in their pedagogical and didactic concept This makes the documentation of the learner's achievements much more appropriate. The connection of the Portfolio approach with the concept of Open Badges makes it possible to create a documentation system where teachers and learners get the most comprehensive possible experience using authentic evidence and work Gain a picture of the learner's acquired skills, progress and development motivating and encouraging for the individual. Traditionally existing grading systems in School contexts, on the other hand, have a strong classifying effect and are generalizing in many aspects Individuals' learning achievements are not sufficiently adequate and often have a demotivating effect.

4. As an issuer, do I have to install my own infrastructure to issue badges?

No. The “MINT-O-Badges” platform offers the service for educational organizations (issuers) that they Create and award badges without having to install any software yourself. The login takes place via the access data.

5. How is it ensured that an issuer really belongs to the specified organization?

Currently, access as an issuer is only granted by invitation. This increases trustworthiness the issuer ensures.

6. How are the badges transferred from the issuer to the learners? What data must/am I allowed to use? Raise organization from learners?

Data protection has the highest priority, which is why data is saved as much as possible. The issuer requires from the learner only receives an email address. The badges are saved to this email address.

7. Where are the badges?

The badges are currently in a database at the University of Münster. The plan is to expand the Project and further development of the MVP to find a suitable place for this at any time ensures free access for learners and the ongoing and secure preservation and storage of the Badges guaranteed.
